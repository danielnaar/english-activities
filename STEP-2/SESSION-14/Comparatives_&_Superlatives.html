Comparatives & Superlatives

The ultimate executive guide to comparisons. Master core formations, advanced patterns, and avoid common structural mistakes.

Core Formation

Syllable Rules

Rule of thumb: Short words (-er/-est) vs. Long words (more/most)

1 Syllable

Add -er / -est

brave braver, bravest
small smaller, smallest
dark darker, darkest

2 Syllables (-y, -le, -er)

Also take -er / -est

happy happier, happiest
clever cleverer, cleverest
simple simpler, simplest

3+ Syllables

Use more / most

beautiful more / most beautiful
expensive more / most expensive

Adverbs (-ly)

Use more / most

quickly more / most quickly
carefully more / most carefully

Spelling Rules

-y to -ier / -iest busy ➔ busier
Drop final -e wide ➔ wider
Double consonant (CVC) big ➔ bigger

Note: Two-syllable adjectives like quiet, narrow, polite allow both forms (e.g., friendlier OR more friendly).

Advanced Patterns

Equality & Inequality

as + adjective + as

"This laptop is as light as yours."

not as / so + adjective + as

"The blue one is not as cheap as the green one."

Modifiers & Growth

much / a lot / far / slightly + comparative

"The train is far faster than the bus."

more and more / adj-er and adj-er

"She is more and more confident."

"Days are shorter and shorter."

The Double Comparative (Cause & Effect)

The comparative ..., the comparative ...
  • "The more you practice, the better you sound."
  • "The sooner we start, the easier it gets."
  • "The less noise, the more I can focus."

Superlatives in Context

Groups & Articles

Everest is the highest mountain.

He is the tallest in the class.

This is the cutest of all the puppies.

Ever / So Far

This is the best movie I have ever seen.

It is the most useful tip so far.

By Far / Easily (Emphasis)

She is by far the most creative designer on the team.

Executive Precision

Frequent Mistakes

Double Comparatives
✕ more better
✓ better
✕ more happier
✓ happier
Missing 'Than'

Always use than when comparing two subjects.

✓ She is taller than me.

Note: Both "taller than me" and "taller than I am" are correct.

Superlative + Plural
✓ One of the most useful ideas.

Tricky Words & Style

● Absolute Adjectives

Avoid comparing absolutes: unique, perfect, dead, empty, impossible.

✕more perfect
✓absolutely perfect
● Time and Order
Late ➔ Later
Superlatives: • latest (newest)
• last (final)
Near ➔ Nearer
Superlatives: • nearest (distance)
• next (order)
