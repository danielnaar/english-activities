Prepositions of Time:
at - in - on

A quick map of when to use each one, plus the most common exceptions.

The Big Idea

AT

Precise Time

  • •at 3 o'clock, at 10:30 a.m.
  • •at noon, at midnight
  • •at lunchtime, at dinnertime, at bedtime
  • •at sunrise, at sunset
  • •at the moment

Example

The train leaves at 6:45 p.m.

IN

Months, Years, Periods

  • •in May, in July
  • •in 1990, in the 1990s
  • •in summer / in the summer
  • •in the morning / afternoon / evening
  • •in the next century

Example

I started university in 2022.

ON

Days & Dates

  • •on Sunday, on Mondays
  • •on 6 March, on July 4
  • •on Christmas Day, on New Year's Eve
  • •on my birthday
  • •on Monday morning / on Saturday nights

Example

See you on Tuesday.

Fixed Expressions

Expression Example
at night The stars shine at night.
at the weekend (BrE) on the weekend (AmE) I usually relax at the weekend / on the weekend.
at Christmas / at Easter We visit family at Christmas.
at the same time We finished at the same time.
at present He is not home at present.

Note: Use "at" for many set phrases that feel like one unit.

Executive Nuances

✕ No preposition

With last, next, every, this we do not use at/in/on.

  • • I went to London last June. (not in last June)
  • • He is coming back next Tuesday. (not on next Tuesday)
  • • We go home every Easter.

On time vs in time

on time = punctual, not late.

in time = early enough to do something.

"The meeting started on time, and I arrived in time to print the slides."

Combine day + part of day

Use on when a day comes first, and in for the part of the day by itself.

in the morning → on Tuesday morning

One-Page Cheat Sheet

at

precise time

  • at 7:15, at noon, at midnight
  • at lunchtime, at sunrise
  • at the moment
in

months & years

  • in April, in 2025, in the 90s
  • long periods: in winter, in the future
  • parts of day: in the evening
on

days & dates

  • on Friday, on 12 June
  • named days: on Christmas Day
  • day + part: on Sunday morning
