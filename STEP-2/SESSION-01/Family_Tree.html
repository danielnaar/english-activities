Executive Edge: Complex Cases

The "No-Translation" Relatives

In Spanish, we have specific words for complex marital ties, but in English, they do not exist. You must explain the relationship literally:

Consuegro / Consuegra

"My son's father-in-law"
or simply "My son's wife's parents"

Concuñado / Concuñada

"My sister-in-law's husband"
or "My husband's brother's wife"

Family Story

Jack is married to Alice. She is his wife and he is her husband. They have two children: a son, Henry, and a daughter, Lisa. They live next to Alice's parents, Harry and Marjorie. Harry is Alice's father and Marjorie is her mother.

For Jack, Harry is his father-in-law and Marjorie is his mother-in-law. Henry is Harry's grandson and Lisa is Marjorie's granddaughter.

Alice has two siblings: her sister, Mary, and her brother, Frank. Frank has two children, David and Sherrie. Sherrie is Alice's niece and David is Alice's nephew. Alice is their aunt and Jack is their uncle.

Extra: David and Sherrie are cousins of Henry and Lisa.
