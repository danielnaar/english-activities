Could in the past

Master the past tense of CAN. Learn how to accurately express past abilities and possibilities, and understand the critical exception for specific occasions.

Structural Framework

Affirmative

Subject + could + base verb
"When I was 10, I could swim very well."

Negative

Subject + couldn't + base verb
"She couldn't drive in 2015."

Questions

Could + subject + base verb?
"Could they play the piano?"

The Base Verb Rule

The "base verb" means the pure dictionary form (play, swim, speak).
Avoid: played speaks to play

Applications

1. Past Ability

Refers to a general ability or habit during a period in the past.

  • • "When I lived in Japan, I couldn't speak Japanese..."
  • • "My grandma could bake amazing cakes."
2. Past Possibility

It was possible (or impossible) at that time due to rules, money, transport, etc.

  • • "We couldn't go to China, but we could go to Korea."

Warning: Specific Occasions

For one successful moment in the past, do not use could. Prefer was/were able to or managed to.

✕ Yesterday I could open the door. (Sounds odd to native speakers)
✓ Yesterday I was able to open the door.
✓ Yesterday I managed to open the door.
Exception: For negative situations on specific occasions, couldn't is perfectly correct:
"There was a bus strike, so I couldn't arrive on time."
