Future Intentions:
To Be Going To

Express your future plans, scheduled projects, and strategic intentions with absolute structural clarity.

Structural Framework

We use be going to to express future plans and intentions. Consider these primary examples:

  • ✓ "My mother is going to buy a car next week."
  • ✓ "They are going to visit Hamilton in December."

Affirmative

Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb
"I am going to study."

Negative

Subject + am/is/are not + going to + base verb
"She isn't going to travel."

Questions

Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + base verb?
"Are they going to play?"

The "To Go" Redundancy

If the main verb is "to go", do not repeat it twice. Avoid saying "going to go". Instead, use the continuous form directly to improve your conversational flow and sound more natural.

Correct Usage Transformations

✕ I am going to go home right now.
✓ I'm going home right now.
✕ She is going to go swimming tomorrow.
✓ She's going swimming tomorrow.
