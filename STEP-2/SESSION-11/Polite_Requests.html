Polite Requests

Master the art of asking. Use these modal verbs to gracefully ask people to do things or to seek professional permission.

The Executive Formality Scale

The more formal or polite you want to sound, the more you should escalate through these modales:

Can Informal
Could Polite
Would Formal

Modal Matrix

Can

Friendly • Informal

Best used with close colleagues, friends, or direct reports.

"Can you help me lift this box?"

Could

Softer • More Polite

The standard choice for professional requests in the workplace.

"Could you explain this exercise?"

Would

Very Polite • Formal

Reserved for clients, high-level management, or delicate requests.

"Would you give me her number, please?"

Will

Neutral • Direct

Used to ask for commitments or give direct instructions smoothly.

"Will you pay by card?"

May

Permission • Highly Formal

Strictly used for asking permission from superiors or in formal settings.

(Note: Usually only used with 'I' or 'We').

"May I use your pen?"
"May I leave early today?"
