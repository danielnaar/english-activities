Telling the Time

Two clear ways to say the time in English, with examples you can use right now.

Asking the time

  • •What time is it?
  • •What's the time?
  • •Do you have the time?
  • •Have you got the right time?
  • •What time do you make it?

Friendly add-ons:

please, excuse me, sorry to bother you.

2 Two Systems

past / to

  • Minutes past the hour for :01 to :29.
  • Minutes to the next hour for :31 to :59.
  • Special words: o'clock (exact hour), half past (:30), a quarter past (:15), a quarter to (:45).

digital

Say the numbers:
three fifteen, three fifty.

You can add a.m. / p.m. or use the 24-hour clock: 15:50.

How to say it

Time past / to System Digital System
3:00 It is three o'clock. three o'clock / three
3:05 Five past three. three oh five
3:10 Ten past three. three ten
3:15 A quarter past three. three fifteen
3:30 Half past three. three thirty
3:45 A quarter to four. three forty-five
3:50 Ten to four. three fifty
3:58 Nearly four o'clock. three fifty-eight
Tip:

In some areas, people also say quarter of for a quarter to. Both are understood.

Quick Rules

•

Use past for minutes 1...29 after the hour: twenty past six.

•

Use to for minutes to the next hour: twenty to seven (6:40).

•

half past = :30
a quarter past = :15
a quarter to = :45

•

o'clock for exact hours. For zero minutes in digital, say oh: three oh five.

a.m. = morning
p.m. = afternoon/evening
00:00 is midnight, 12:00 is noon.

Sound natural

  • about / around five thirty ≈ 5:30
  • nearly / almost four a little before 4:00
  • just gone three a little after 3:00
  • exactly / on the dot seven 7:00 sharp

Prepositions

Use at for times: at 6, at noon, at midnight.

Combine with days: See you on Friday at 8 p.m.
