Possessive Pronouns

They replace a noun phrase and show ownership or professional relationship. Use them to avoid repetition when the noun is clearly understood from context.

The Structural Patterns

1
Standalone Possession

Subject + be + possessive pronoun

Do not repeat the noun. Let the pronoun stand alone.

  • ✓That red bike is mine.
  • ✓The corner office is ours.
  • ✓Is this seat yours?

2
The "Of" Relationship

noun + of + possessive pronoun

To show relationships with singular count nouns.

  • ✓She is a colleague of theirs.
  • ✓I met a friend of mine.

The Set

Person Pronoun Contextual Example
1st singular mine This ticket is mine.
2nd sing. / plural yours Are these headphones yours?
3rd singular (male) his The idea was his.
3rd singular (female) hers The victory is hers.
1st plural ours The corner office is ours.
3rd plural theirs The decision is theirs.

Crucial Distinctions

Adjectives vs. Pronouns

Adjectives go before a noun. Pronouns replace the noun entirely.

Adjective Pronoun
my book ➔ mine
their house ➔ theirs

The "its" Exception

its has no standalone pronoun form. It only functions as a determiner before a noun.

✕ This tail is its.
✓ The tail is the animal's.

No Apostrophes

Possessive pronouns never take apostrophes. Do not confuse possession with contractions.

✕ your's / her's
✓ yours / hers

it's = it is / it has
its = possession (e.g. its paws)
