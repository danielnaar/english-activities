Present Progressive

Also known as Present Continuous. Use it to communicate actions in progress, short-term situations, fixed arrangements, and changing corporate trends.

Structural Form
am / is / are + verb-ing

• I am working
• He/She/It is studying
• You/We/They are cooking

Spelling Adjustments (verb + ing)

Change ie ➔ y lie ➔ lying
Drop silent 'e' make ➔ making
CVC + Stress run ➔ running

The 6 Applications

1
Happening Now

Actions occurring at this exact moment of speaking.

"Maya is answering an email right now."

2
Fixed Arrangements

Timetabled or agreed plans happening in the near future.

"We are meeting Ana at 7 tonight."

3
Temporary Actions

Short-term, non-permanent professional situations.

"His father is working in Rome this month."

4
Around The Moment

Longer actions happening 'these days' but not necessarily right now.

"My friend is preparing for his exams."

5
Trends and Change

Developing situations, market habits, or changing metrics.

"Streaming is growing fast."

6
Repeated Annoyances

Use with always, constantly, forever to express professional irritation.

"Andrew is always coming late."

Signal Words

•now / right now
•at the moment
•today / this week
•currently
•these days
•tonight / tomorrow
For Annoyances: always, constantly, forever

Quick Reminders

✕ Do not use with Stative Verbs

Verbs of state, possession or feeling cannot take the -ing form.

know, love, believe, understand, want
I am knowing ➔ I know

ℹ Near-Future Plans

Both 'be going to' and the Present Progressive are structurally valid for expressing arranged future plans.
