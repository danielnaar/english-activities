The Art of Invitations

Master how to invite, accept, and respectfully decline. Learn the formulas that distinguish native fluency and tactful professional networking.

The Three Pillars

1

Making Invitations

Formula: Invite + Details + Softener. Give clear purpose, time, and location.

  • ● "Would you like to come to my birthday on Friday?"
  • ● "How about coming to the game this weekend?"
  • ● "We are grilling later. You in?"
2

Accepting

Natural Add-ons: Express enthusiasm and confirm commitment smoothly.

  • ● "Sounds great. See you then."
  • ● "Awesome, I am in. Can I bring anything?"
  • ● "Thank you for the invitation. I would love to attend."
3

Politely Refusing

Soft Refusal: Thanks + Reason + Alternative. Protect the relationship.

  • ● "Thanks, but I have to pass this time. Rain check?"
  • ● "Wish I could, but I am tied up tonight. Next time."
  • ● "I would like to come, but I am busy that day."

Fluency & Culture

Textbook vs. Modern Native

"Textbook" English Modern Native phrasing
"I would like to invite you to..." "We are doing [X], want to join?"
"Could you come to my party?" "Do you want to come to my party?"
"I cannot go because I am busy." "I have plans that day. Can we do next week?"
