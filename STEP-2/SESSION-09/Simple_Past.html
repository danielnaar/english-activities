The Simple Past

Communicate finished actions in a finished time. A smooth, professional guide to structural accuracy, irregular patterns, and precise pronunciation.

Structural Framework

Affirmative

Subject + past verb (regular +ed / irregular special)
"I visited Tokyo in 2019."

Negative

Subject + didn't + base verb
"They didn't visit the castle."

Questions

[Wh-] + did + subject + base verb?
"When did she arrive?"

Warning: Only One Past Marker

Use the past form on the main verb OR use did + base verb. Never mix both. Keep the base form after did/didn't.

✕ I did went.
✓ I went. / I didn't go.

Applications

1. Finished Action

"Yesterday I lost my keys."

2. Sequence of Events

"He opened the door, looked inside, and smiled."

3. Past Facts / States

"She lived in Peru in 2015."

4. Past Habits (with Adverbs)

"We often played outside as kids."

Time Markers

yesterday last night last week in 2010 an hour ago on Monday
Adverb Placement
  • just / only / never: Before main verb.
    "I just called you."
  • In questions: After the subject.
    "Did you just call?"

Irregular Verbs Pattern Map

Grouping irregular verbs by their transformation pattern accelerates memorization.

Pattern Group Examples
Vowel Change drink ➔ drank
speak ➔ spoke
Same Form put ➔ put
cost ➔ cost
-ought / -aught bring ➔ brought
buy ➔ bought
The "Fun" Set go ➔ went
make ➔ made

Special Case: The Verb 'To Be'

I / He / She / It was (wasn't)
You / We / They were (weren't)
DO NOT use 'did' with BE
✕ Did you were late?
✓ Were you late?

Pronunciation & Spelling Mechanics

How does '-ed' sound?

/t/

After voiceless sounds

kissed
watched
worked

/d/

After voiced sounds

played
rained
called

/id/

Only after 't' or 'd'

wanted
ended

Spelling Adjustments

Verb ends in 'e' live ➔ lived
Consonant + 'y' ➔ 'ied' study ➔ studied
Short vowel + Consonant (CVC) stop ➔ stopped
Exceptions (Both OK) traveled / travelled
