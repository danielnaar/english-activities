Too vs. Enough

Negative vs. positive balance: Express when something is 'more than necessary' or when it is 'just right'.

Too ➔ Negative

It means 'more than necessary'.

Too + adjective / adverb
  • • Use too before adjectives and adverbs.
✓ This coffee is too hot. I can't drink it.
too cold too expensive too quickly

Enough ➔ Positive

It means 'sufficient, adequate'.

adjective / adverb + enough enough + noun
  • • Use enough after adjectives/adverbs, but before nouns.
✓ The weather is warm enough for a picnic.
✓ There's enough food in the fridge.
fast enough enough chairs big enough

Quick Note

Tone & Context

"Too" = negative / "Enough" = positive.

Example Concept

Too broke to buy coffee vs. Rich enough to complain about it.
