Adverbs of Frequency

A definitive guide on how often something happens, proper structural placement, and executive nuances for precise communication.

The Frequency Scale

% Adverb Example in Context
100% always I always have breakfast.
90% usually We usually buy groceries on Fridays.
80% normally / generally I normally go to church on Sundays.
70% often / frequently She often goes bowling.
50% sometimes I sometimes stay home on Mondays.
30% occasionally We occasionally eat seafood.
10% seldom He seldom watches reality shows.
5% hardly ever / rarely I hardly ever drink coffee.
0% never They never drink alcohol.

* Percentages are approximate conceptual markers.

Where do we put the adverb?

1
Before a main verb

Subject + adverb + main verb
  • ✓I usually eat at 7.
  • ✓She often studies at night.

2
After the verb 'to be'

Subject + am/is/are + adverb
  • ✓They are never late.
  • ✓He is usually calm.

3
With auxiliaries or modals

Subject + aux + adverb + main verb
  • ✓You can sometimes park here.
  • ✓We have often visited Toronto.
  • ✓They might never agree.

4
Continuous forms

be + adverb + verb-ing
  • ✓She is always checking her phone.
  • ✓I am rarely running late.

Sentence Starters

✓ These are fine

Usually, normally, often, frequently, sometimes, occasionally.

  • • Sometimes, I cook Thai food.
  • • Usually, we walk to school.

✕ Do not start a sentence with:

Always, seldom, rarely, hardly ever, never.

✕ Always, I wake up at 5. ➔ ✓ I always wake up at 5.
✕ Never I eat sugar. ➔ ✓ I never eat sugar.

Be More Specific

Use these expressions when you want to communicate exact frequency instead of an approximation:

every day
every Monday
once a week
twice a month
three times a year
every other day
