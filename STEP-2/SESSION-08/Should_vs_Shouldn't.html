Should & Shouldn't

Master the art of giving advice, making polite suggestions, and expressing professional expectations with perfect structural accuracy.

The Structural Framework

Affirmative

Subject + should + base verb
"You should drink more water."

Negative

Subject + shouldn't + base verb
"He shouldn't drive so fast."

Questions

Should + subject + base verb?
"Should we book a table?"

The Golden Rule

After should or shouldn't, always use the base verb.
No "to", no "-s", no "-ed". ✓ should go, ✓ should study.

Professional Contexts

● Advice

Soft suggestion / Friendly tone

"You should see a doctor."

"You shouldn't skip breakfast."

● Opinion & Recommendation

Often paired with 'I think'

"I think we should start earlier."

"I don't think you should buy it now."

● Expectation & Probability

Not 100% certain, but probable

"The bus should arrive at 7."

"It shouldn't take long."

Common Errors to Avoid

✕ He shoulds study more.
✓ He should study more.
✕ You should to practice every day.
✓ You should practice every day.
✕ Do you should call your mom?
✓ Should you call your mom?
✕ I don't should eat late.
✓ I shouldn't eat late.
Pronunciation

The 'L' is silent.

should = /ʃʊd/
shouldn't = /ʃʊdənt/

Tip: say "shood"
With Adverbs

Place adverbs like probably, really, definitely after the subject or after should.

  • ✓ You should probably call.
  • ✓ You probably should call.

Modal Power Scale

Understand the exact severity of the modal verb you choose to use in the workplace.

Should

Soft advice / expectation

"You should wear a helmet."

Ought to

Similar to should, more formal

"You ought to wear a helmet."

Had better

Strong advice + warning

"You had better wear a helmet."

Must / Have to

Strong obligation / strict rule

"You must wear a helmet."

✦ Advanced Note

Past Regrets / Criticism:

Form: should have + past participle

  • "You should have called me."
  • "You shouldn't have shouted."
