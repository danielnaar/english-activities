Advanced Quantifiers

A quick guide for quantity words. Master the executive formula:
Noun Type + Sentence Type = Your Best Choice.

The Core Uses

[U] Uncountable [C] Countable Plural
Word Use With Typical Use Contextual Example
much [U] Questions / Negatives. "Do you have much time?"
many [C] Questions / Negatives. "There are not many buses."
a lot of [U] or [C] Neutral / Affirmative. "We have a lot of homework."
lots of [U] or [C] Informal, equivalent to 'a lot of'. "There are lots of apples."
a little [U] Small amount, positive meaning. "There is a little milk."
a few [C] Small number, positive meaning. "I have a few friends."

Key Considerations

Watch Out

Never mix a countable quantifier with an uncountable noun, or vice versa.

✕ much apples
➔ many apples
✕ many money
➔ much money
✕ lots coffee
➔ lots of coffee

The "A" makes a difference

Using little and few without the "a" conveys a negative or insufficient meaning.

  • ● We have little time. Meaning: We have almost none. (Negative tone)
  • ● I have few friends here. Meaning: I do not have many. (Negative tone)
