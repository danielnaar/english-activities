Use of -ing

One ending, different roles in English. Master how the -ing suffix transforms verbs into nouns, adjectives, or complements to build sophisticated sentences.

The Three Roles

● As a Noun

Acts as the subject or object of a sentence. It names an activity rather than doing it.

"Singing is good for your health."
"Swimming is my favorite sport."

● As an Adjective

Describes a noun. It tells us about the characteristic or effect of a person, place, or thing.

"It was a boring movie."
"I had a hard working day."

● As part of a Verb

Follows certain main verbs (like enjoy, hate, finish, stop) to complete the action.

"I hate smoking."
"She enjoys reading."

Executive Context

Corporate English

Using -ing as a noun is extremely common in business settings to sound more objective and formal.

Subject Noun "Networking is essential for career growth."
After a verb "Management suggested delaying the launch."
