Present Simple
vs. Progressive

Daily routines vs. Actions happening right now. Master the distinction to accurately convey timing and habits in professional environments.

Structural Forms

Present Simple

+
Affirmative
Subject + base / 3rd person -s

She sings every day.

-
Negative
Subject + do/does not + base

She does not sing often.

?
Question
Do/Does + subject + base?

Does she sing well?

Present Progressive

+
Affirmative
Subject + be + verb-ing

She is singing now.

-
Negative
Subject + be not + verb-ing

She is not singing now.

?
Question
Be + subject + verb-ing?

Is she singing now?

What are they for?

Present Simple

  • •
    Habits and routines "I drink coffee every morning."
  • •
    General facts "The sun rises in the east."
Time Markers
always usually often sometimes never every day

Present Progressive

  • •
    Actions happening now "She is studying for an exam."
  • •
    Temporary actions "They are living in Paris this year."
Time Markers
now right now at the moment this week currently today

Executive Precision

Contrast in Context

Simple He plays the guitar every weekend.
Prog. He is playing the guitar right now.
Simple I work in a bank.
Prog. I am working from home today.

Common Mistakes

Don't mix up routine vs current action.

✕ She is sing every day.
✓ She sings every day.
✕ They go to school right now.
✓ They are going to school right now.
