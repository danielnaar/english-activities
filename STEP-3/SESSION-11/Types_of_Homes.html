Homes Around the World

Discovering Diversity in Living Spaces

Read about different types of homes. Pay attention to how they are described using comparative and superlative adjectives.

1

Apartment in the City

City apartments are usually smaller than houses, but they are more convenient for people who love energy and activity. Apartments are often modern, comfortable, and safe, though sometimes a bit noisy or crowded. Living in a city apartment can be more expensive than living in the countryside, but it's also closer to work, cafés, and entertainment.

2

Country House

A country house is usually larger and more peaceful than any city apartment. The air feels fresher, and life moves slower. These homes are often cosy, surrounded by green hills or wide fields. Although life there can be quieter, it may be less convenient because stores and schools are farther away.

3

Beach House

A beach house is one of the most relaxing places to live. The sound of the waves is softer than city traffic, and sunsets are more unforgettable. Beach houses are often bright, airy, and open. However, they can also be more fragile against strong winds and salt air, so they require more maintenance.

4

Mountain Cabin

Mountain cabins are usually smaller but cosier than other homes. They are warmer inside, especially with a fireplace. Life there feels simpler and more peaceful. The view is always amazing—snow, pine trees, and silence make it a perfect place to rest.

5

Tiny House

Tiny houses are becoming more popular because they are cheaper, simpler, and more eco-friendly. They prove that "less can be more". Even though space is limited, clever design makes them comfortable and unique. A tiny home can be as special as a mansion—just smaller and smarter.
