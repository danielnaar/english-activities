Describing Places

Vocabulary & Application Guide

Use advanced adjectives to describe cities, towns, or any place you know. Practice their meanings, pronunciation, and how to combine them naturally.

2

Grammar Focus

Position

Adjectives generally go before nouns or after the verb "be".

It's a beautiful city.

(Before noun)

The city is beautiful.

(After 'be')
3

Examples in Context

Ancient

Rome is full of ancient buildings.

Cosy

We stayed in a cosy cabin near the lake.

Peaceful

Kyoto temples are peaceful even when crowded.

Crowded

The market was too crowded to move around.

Unforgettable

Our trip to Iceland was unforgettable!

Dangerous

That mountain road can be dangerous at night.

4

Challenge / Extension

Combine Adjectives

Elevate the language by linking two descriptors together to create a richer image.

  • ➔ a small peaceful village
  • ➔ an ancient narrow street
  • ➔ a safe and comfortable place to live
