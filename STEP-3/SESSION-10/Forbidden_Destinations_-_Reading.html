Forbidden Destinations
Grammar in Context

The Arch of Ctesiphon - The world's largest brick-built arch near Baghdad, Iraq.

Around the world, travelers sometimes have to accept that not every amazing place is open to visit. In Iraq, people can't easily go to the Arch of Ctesiphon, even though it's one of the most impressive ancient structures.

In Iran, tourists must follow strict visa and clothing rules to see places like Isfahan. Those who visit Libya must not explore freely because some areas remain unsafe.

Travelers don't have to worry about beauty—these countries already have incredible landscapes and monuments—but they need to think carefully before planning a trip.

The Hoggar Mountains in Algeria are spectacular, yet visitors mustn't go there without special permission. In the Philippines, people need to avoid parts of Mindanao for security reasons. These destinations remind us that we have to respect safety rules, even when our curiosity wants to explore.
