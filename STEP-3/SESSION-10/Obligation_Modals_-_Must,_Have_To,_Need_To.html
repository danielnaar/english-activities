Necessity & Obligation
Have to, Must & Need to

These three verbs are often used to express necessity or obligation, but each one carries a different tone or strength. Knowing when to use them will make your English sound more natural and precise.

HAVE TO

External Rules

Used for external obligations: rules, schedules, or responsibilities decided by others.

  • •

    "I have to wear a uniform at work."

  • •

    "She has to wake up early for school."

Fact: Think of it as a "rule" you cannot control. It is impersonal.

MUST

Personal Obligation

Used for strong personal obligation or when the speaker insists on something important.

  • •

    "You must study harder if you want to pass."

  • •

    "I must remember to call Grandma today."

Fact: It feels much more urgent and emotional than "have to".

NEED TO

Practical Reason

Used when something is necessary or when there's a highly practical reason to do it.

  • •

    "I need to wash my dirty clothes."

  • •

    "You need to rest after such a long day."

Fact: It focuses much more on usefulness rather than obligation.

Common
Mistakes

To Avoid

I must to go now.
I must go now.
You don't must do it.
You don't have to do it.
I need wash my car.
I need to wash my car.
