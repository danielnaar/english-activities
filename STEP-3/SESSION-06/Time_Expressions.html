Time Expressions

Master the specific keywords used with the Present Perfect. Understand their logic to connect the past with the present perfectly.

Since

Refers to the exact starting point of an action. It answers the question "From what point in time?".

Usage

Use since with a specific year or period in the past.

- I have lived in Toronto since 2010.

- She has studied English since she was a child.

For

Measures the duration or total length of an action. It answers the question "How long?".

Usage

Use for with a number of years or a period of time.

- They have worked here for twenty years.

- We have waited for the bus for half an hour.

? Ever & Yet

Ever: Means "at any time in your life". Usually goes in questions.

Yet: Means "up to now". Used at the very end of negative sentences and questions.

Usage

Use ever and yet in questions and negatives.

- Have you ever traveled to Japan?

- He hasn't finished his homework yet.

Already & Never

Already: Shows an action happened sooner than expected.

Never: Means "at no time in the past". It makes a positive grammatical sentence have a negative meaning.

Usage

Use already and never between have/has and the main verb.

- She has already visited that museum twice.

- I have never eaten sushi before.

✨ Other Expressions

Use before, many times, so far and others to expand context.

So far: Means "up to this moment" and implies the situation might continue.

- Have you been to this restaurant before?

- I have seen that movie many times so far.
