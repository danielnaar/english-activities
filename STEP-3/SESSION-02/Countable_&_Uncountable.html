Countable & Uncountable

Not everything in English can be counted... at least not directly! Learn to distinguish and quantify correctly.

The Core Distinction

1, 2, 3... Countable

Things you can count individually using numbers.

• 1 book, 2 books, 3 books...
• 1 friend, 2 friends, 3 friends...
Executive Tip

Use many with countables.

"We have many teachers."

✕ Uncountable

Substances, concepts, or liquids you cannot count with numbers.

✓
I have a lot of money.
(Not: 1000 money)
✓
She drinks a lot of milk.
(Not: 5 milk)
Executive Tip

Use much with uncountables.

"We bought much rice for the trip."

How to count uncountables?

We use units, containers, or measures instead of counting the word itself.

Correct Measurement Incorrect Form
✓ 30 euros/dollars ✕ 30 money
✓ 3 liters/glasses of milk ✕ 3 milk
✓ 2 kilos of meat ✕ 2 meat
✓ 8 bottles of water ✕ 8 waters

Common Traps & Mistakes

✕ I have 100 moneys.
✓ I have a lot of money.
✕ She bought 3 milks.
✓ She bought 3 bottles of milk.
✕
He ordered 2 chickens. (Meaning animals, not food)
✓ He ordered 2 plates of chicken.
