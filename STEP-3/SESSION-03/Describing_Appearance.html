Describing Appearance

Learn the essential vocabulary to describe how people look. The golden rule is knowing when to use BE and when to use HAVE.

The Golden Rule

BE

General State

Height • Build • Age

"She is tall and slim."
HAVE

Specific Features

Hair • Eyes • Extras

"He has blue eyes."

Vocabulary Categories

Height

BE
tall short medium
  • "She is tall like a basketball player."
  • "He is short, but very strong."
  • "They are medium height."

Build

BE
slim athletic overweight
  • "She is slim and elegant."
  • "He is muscular and goes to the gym."
  • "They are a little overweight."

Hair

HAVE
long short curly straight wavy
  • "She has long straight hair."
  • "He has short curly hair."
  • "My friend has wavy brown hair."

Age

BE
young middle-aged elderly
  • "She is young, maybe 20 years old."
  • "He is middle-aged, around 45."
  • "They are elderly."

Eyes

HAVE
blue brown green hazel
  • "She has blue eyes."
  • "He has big brown eyes."
  • "I have green eyes."

Extras

HAVE / WEAR
beard mustache glasses tattoo
  • "He has a beard and a mustache."
  • "She wears glasses."
  • "He has a tattoo on his arm."

Asking Questions

The Ultimate Formula

What does she look like?

To ask about someone's general physical appearance, we use DO / DOES + LOOK LIKE. Do not use the verb 'to be' for this question.

✕ Incorrect How is she look?
✓ Correct What do they look like?
