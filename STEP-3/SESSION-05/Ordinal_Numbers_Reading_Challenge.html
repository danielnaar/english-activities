Unusual Festivals

Read about these unusual festivals around the world. Then answer the questions using ordinal numbers.

1

Pingxi Lantern Festival (Taiwan)

Every year, people in Taiwan celebrate a beautiful festival of light. They write their wishes and dreams on paper lanterns. Then, they release the lanterns into the night sky. The sky looks amazing, full of thousands of floating lights. People believe the lanterns take their messages to the gods. It is a quiet, magical, and very peaceful event.

2

Harbin Ice Festival (China)

In winter, the city of Harbin becomes a freezing wonderland. Artists use blocks of ice from a frozen river to build huge castles, towers, and animals. At night, they put colorful lights inside the ice. It looks like a city made of beautiful glass. It is very cold, but millions of people visit to see this incredible winter magic.

3

International Kite Festival (India)

In the state of Gujarat, January is a time to look up. During this festival, millions of colorful kites fly in the sky. People of all ages go to their roofs to fly kites and compete with their friends. They eat special sweets and enjoy the warm weather. The sky is completely full of kites from morning until night.

4

The Flower Carpet (Belgium)

Every two years in August, the center of Brussels changes completely. People create a giant carpet on the ground using more than 500,000 real flowers. They work very fast to put the colorful flowers in beautiful patterns. The result is a massive piece of art. Tourists love taking pictures from the balconies around the main square.

5

Songkran Water Festival (Thailand)

April is the hottest month in Thailand, so people cool down with a giant water fight! Songkran is the traditional Thai New Year. People use buckets, water guns, and hoses to throw water at each other in the streets. It is a very happy celebration. The water means washing away bad luck and starting the new year fresh and clean.
