Simple Past vs Past Progressive

Master the difference between completed actions and actions in progress. Daily routines, free time & vacations.

Simple Past

Verb + ed (regular) / irregular

  • • Completed actions in the past
  • • One-time events
  • • Short, finished stories

I swam in the ocean last summer.

She danced at the party.

We visited Paris in 2022.

Past Progressive

was/were + verb + ing

  • • Actions in progress at a past moment
  • • Interrupted actions
  • • Background stories

I was swimming when it started raining.

She was dancing when the music stopped.

We were visiting Paris when the strike began.

Quick Tips

Use Simple Past for finished actions. Use Past Progressive for actions in progress.

Mix them: "While I was watching Netflix, my mom called me."

Common Mistakes

I was went to the beach.
I went to the beach.
I swam when it was raining.
I was swimming when it was raining.
They were play football yesterday.
They played football yesterday.
