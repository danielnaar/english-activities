Comparatives & Superlatives

Grammar Tip & Application Rules

Master the fundamental rules for comparing two things or expressing the highest degree of a quality. Precision is key.

The 4 Golden Rules
Rule 01

Short Adjectives

For short adjectives, add the suffix -er for comparatives and -est for superlatives.

Cheap ➔ Cheaper ➔ The cheapest
Rule 02

Long Adjectives

For long adjectives, use more for comparatives and most for superlatives.

Expensive ➔ More expensive ➔ The most expensive
Rule 03

Comparing Two Things

Use the word "than" immediately after the adjective when you are comparing two things.

"This hotel is bigger than the other one."
Rule 04

The Highest Degree

Use the definite article "the" before the adjective when expressing the highest degree (superlative).

"This is the most comfortable room in the resort."
