EXECUTIVE GRAMMAR MODULE

Modal Verbs
Ability, Permission & Possibility

Master the nuances of Can, Could, May, and Be Able To to elevate the politeness and accuracy of your professional communication.

CAN

Present / Future

Used for ability, permission, and informal requests.

  • "I can swim very fast." (Ability)

  • "Can I borrow your phone?" (Permission)

  • "Can you open the window, please?" (Request)

Common Mistake

Saying "I can to swim."
➔ Correct: I can swim.

Fact: In questions, "Can" sounds friendlier than "May".

COULD

Past / Polite

Polite way to ask for something or to express past ability.

  • "Could you help me with my luggage, please?" (Polite Request)

  • "When I was a child, I could run faster." (Past Ability)

Common Mistake

"I could to run."
➔ Remove "to" after modal verbs. Correct: I could run.

Fact: "Could" is also used for possibility: "It could rain later."

MAY

Formal / Possibility

Used for formal permission or possibility.

  • "May I see your passport, please?" (Formal Request)

  • "It may snow tonight." (Possibility)

Common Mistake

Don't use "May" for general ability. Say "can", not "may", in "I can speak French."

Fact: In spoken English, "May I" is formal; "Can I" is more common in daily speech.

BE ABLE TO

All Tenses

Used for ability in any tense (past, present, future).

  • "I am able to finish this project today." (Present)

  • "She was able to fix the printer." (Past)

  • "We will be able to travel again soon." (Future)

Common Mistake

"I can to do it."
➔ Correct: I can do it. or I am able to do it.

Fact: "Be able to" is used where "can" can't be: "I haven't been able to sleep lately."

Quick Review & Golden Rules

  • Can ability / permission (informal)
  • Could past ability / polite request
  • May formal permission / possibility
  • Be able to all tenses, specific ability

"Be able to" works with all tenses; "Can" only with present and future.
"I can travel tomorrow" = "I will be able to travel tomorrow"

Don't mix them!
"I will can go" ➔ Wrong.
Say: "I will be able to go."
