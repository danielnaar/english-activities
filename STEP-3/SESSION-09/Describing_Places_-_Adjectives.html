EXECUTIVE VOCABULARY MODULE

Describing Places
Vocabulary Guide

Go beyond basic words! Learn to describe cities, towns, landscapes, and experiences vividly to elevate your professional communication.

Positive Feelings

Wonderful

full of beauty or joy

"We had a wonderful time in Florence."

Unforgettable

so special you'll always remember it

"The view from the hill was unforgettable."

Peaceful

calm, quiet, relaxing

"It's a peaceful village by the lake."

Comfortable / Cosy

pleasant and warm

"Our hotel room was cosy and clean."

Safe

not dangerous

"Tokyo is one of the safest cities in the world."

Suitable

good for a particular purpose

"The town is suitable for families with children."

Historic & Cultural Charm

Ancient

very old, with a long history

"Athens is full of ancient buildings."

Historic

important in history

"We stayed near the historic city center."

Fascinating

extremely interesting

"The museum was absolutely fascinating."

Popular

visited or liked by many people

"Barcelona is a popular tourist destination."

Numerous

many in number

"There are numerous cafés in this street."

Modern & Urban Vibe

Crowded

full of people

"The subway is crowded during rush hour."

Noisy

loud or full of sounds

"Our hotel was next to a noisy street."

Narrow

not wide

"Venice has many narrow canals."

Dangerous

not safe

"It's dangerous to walk there at night."

Modern / Lively

energetic, full of activity

"Tokyo is a modern and lively capital city."

Pro Tips: Using Adjectives Naturally

Collocations

  • •beautiful beach
  • •historic castle
  • •peaceful park
  • •narrow street

Contrast Ideas

"The city is crowded but fascinating."

"The village is small but unforgettable."

Common Mistakes

Don't say "very ancient".
Say "extremely old" instead.

Don't say "more perfect".
Say just "perfect"!
