Grammar Mastery

Describing Experiences

Learn how to tell a great story step by step: plan, grammar, details, and useful phrases.

1. Plan Your Story

When Where Who Goal Obstacle Climax Outcome Reflection
Golden Rule Keep one main line of action. Each sentence should move time forward or add a vivid detail.

2. Verb Tenses

Use Form Mini Example
Main events Past simple I arrived, met the team, and signed in.
Background / Earlier past Past continuous
Past perfect		 People were cheering while we waited.
We had booked everything, so check-in was easy.
Up to now / Duration Present perfect
Past perfect continuous		 I have never felt that proud before.
I had been training for months.
Habits then Used to / would We used to camp by the river.

3. Add Details (The 5 Senses)

Make it Vivid

Saw: glow, blur, skyline
Heard: buzz, chant, crack
Tasted: smoky, bitter, fresh
Smelled: salty air, cinnamon, fuel
Felt: numb fingers, shaky knees

Time Phrases

Last summer Back in 2019 One rainy evening On my first day Minutes before the show

Show, Don't Tell

Tell (Boring) Show (Vivid)
I was nervous. My hands wouldn't stop tapping the rail.
The place was crowded. Shoulders brushed my backpack at every step.
It was unforgettable. The song still echoes when I pass that street.

4. Connecting Words

Order of Events

  • • At first / By then
  • • Afterwards
  • • Meanwhile
  • • Eventually / Suddenly

Reasons & Differences

  • • Because / So
  • • Although / However
  • • In spite of
  • • Despite

Feelings & Reactions

  • • Surprisingly / Incredibly
  • • To my relief
  • • To be honest
  • • Luckily / Oddly enough

5. Useful Words & Phrases

Connectors & Frames

To organize your story

  • To start: "It all started when..." / "To begin with..."
  • During the event: "All of a sudden," / "Out of nowhere,"
  • To highlight: "What stood out the most was..."
  • To reflect: "Looking back,..." / "In the end,..."

Adverbs & Intensifiers

To make feelings stronger

Absolutely e.g., absolutely terrifying
Incredibly e.g., incredibly beautiful
Surprisingly e.g., surprisingly calm
Deeply e.g., deeply moving
Utterly e.g., utterly exhausted

6. Example Story

Last summer, I arrived at a tiny coastal town for a night run.

The boardwalk was buzzing with music and the smell of grilled corn.

By the time the horn blew, I had been training for ten weeks, but the first hill hit like a wall.

Halfway up, a stranger shouted my name from a homemade sign.

Eventually, I crossed the finish line with shaky legs, salt on my lips, and a stupid grin.

I have kept the paper bib in my wallet ever since.

Spot the grammar tools: past simple for events, past continuous for background, past perfect continuous for preparation, present perfect for the lasting result.
