The Marvelous Modal: CAN!

CAN is a superhero among modal verbs. It helps you do things, ask for stuff, and express your powers (or your limitations).

What CAN do?

• Ability

"I can solve a Rubik's Cube in 12 hours! (Hey, it still counts!)"

• Possibility

"It can rain later, but who trusts the weather app?"

• Permission

"Can I eat this last cookie? (Spoiler: You already did.)"

• Request

"Can you fix this? It was working before I touched it."

Structure

Subject + can + base verb

She can dance like nobody's watching.

Negative Form

I can't cook. I microwave with passion, though!

Use can't or cannot (same meaning, different style).

Questions

Can you speak whale? (Like in Finding Nemo?)

Invert the subject and CAN to ask questions. Easy peasy!

Common Mistakes

✕ He cans swim. (CAN never takes an "s"!)
→ ✓ He can swim.

Remember!

  • • CAN is always followed by the base verb (no "to").
  • • It doesn't change for he/she/it. No "cans" or "canned" here!

Beware of other meanings!

CAN is never conjugated like a regular verb. No "cans", no "canned", unless you're talking about soup.

As a Noun

A metal container for soda, soup, or questionable leftovers.

As a Verb (to can)

Means to preserve food. Grandma cans peaches like a boss.

Slang (to fire)

"To can someone" means to fire them. Harsh, but efficient.

Slang (The can)

"The can" is slang for the toilet. Yep, someone thought that was poetic.

Fun Tip

If you say "Can I go to the bathroom?" and the teacher replies, "I don't know, can you?" you've just met a grammar purist.
