PREMIUM ENGLISH • ORAL PRACTICE

What CAN You Do?

Use the modal verb CAN in real, personal, and fun ways during a conversation with your teacher or classmates.

What is an oral practice?

It's a classroom activity designed to help you speak, share, and explore ideas out loud. Your teacher will guide the conversation, but it's up to you to express your thoughts and listen to others.

Don't worry about being perfect; this is about fluency, not perfection!

Icebreaker

"I don't know what I'm good at."

Let's help each other discover our talents and skills!

1. What Can You Do?

  • • Do you know what you are good at?
  • • Do you know your strengths and talents?
  • • It's not only what you like. It's what you can do!
I like singing, but I can't sing well.
I don't like cooking, but I can cook amazing rice!

2. CAN + LIKE Combos

Ask your partner and follow up:

• Do you like singing?

→ Can you sing in tune?

• Do you like traveling?

→ Can you carry your backpack for 5 hours?

• Do you like acting?

→ Can you remember long dialogues?

• Do you like dancing?

→ Can you dance in public?

3. Challenge Questions

  • • Can you run fast, but you hate running?
  • • Can you swim, but are afraid of the ocean?
  • • Can you lie... even if you don't like lying?
  • • Can you dance, but avoid dancing at weddings?

4. Strange CANs!

  • • Can you eat a lemon without making a face?
  • • Can you sleep with the lights on?
  • • Can you talk for 10 minutes without using the word "I"?
  • • Can you fake a laugh that sounds real?

Final Task:
Your "CAN" Résumé

Prepare a short description (real or invented!) that includes at least 5 things you can or can't do.

Present it to a classmate or group.

Example

"Hi, I'm Daniel. I can cook, I can play the guitar, and I can speak three languages. But I can't ride a bike or whistle! I know... weird, right?"
