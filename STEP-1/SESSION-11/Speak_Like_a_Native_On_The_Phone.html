Speak Like a Native:
On the Phone!

Master the casual, natural expressions native speakers use during phone calls. Sound less like a textbook and more like a fluent professional.

1. Greetings & Introductions

  • • "Hi, this is Jake speaking."
  • • "Hey! How's it going?"
  • • "Hi there! Is this Sam?"
  • • "Hey, can you talk right now?"

2. Making a Request

  • • "Can I ask you something real quick?"
  • • "Do you have a second?"
  • • "Would you mind helping me with something?"
  • • "Just wanted to check in about..."

3. Clarifying or Reacting

  • • "Sorry, you cut out for a sec."
  • • "Could you repeat that?"
  • • "Wait, what do you mean exactly?"
  • • "Oh wow, really?!"

4. Ending the Call

  • • "Alright, I'll let you go now."
  • • "Great talking to you!"
  • • "I'll catch you later!"
  • • "Let's talk soon. Take care!"
Phrases to Avoid (Not Natural!)
✕
"Hello, I am John. I want to talk." → Too robotic.
✕
"Please can you help me with my problem?" → Too formal.
✕
"I will call you again later, thank you very much." → Sounds scripted.
✕
"Goodbye." (just that) → Sounds too abrupt or old-fashioned.

Native Phone Tricks

  • • They say: "Just calling to say hi!"
  • • Instead of "Can I speak to Benji", natives say: "Is Benji around?"
  • • They use "wanna", "gonna", "lemme" all the time.
  • • Long silences are okay - you don't have to fill every second.

Fun Fact

In the UK, "ring me" means "call me", nothing to do with jewelry! And Americans say "call you back" even when they never actually called the first time.
