Possessive Adjectives

Use possessive adjectives to show who something belongs to.

Possessive Adjectives Chart

Subject Possessive Adjective Example
I my This is my book.
you your Is that your phone?
he his His name is John.
she her Her bag is pink.
it its The cat licked its paw.
we our Our house is big.
you (plural) your Your children are lovely.
they their Their car is red.

Examples in Context

I have a dog. My dog is friendly.
We are at school. Our teacher is funny.
Mark and Julia are here. Their luggage is heavy.
This is Anna. Her sister is nice.
Look at the cat! Its eyes are blue.

Helpful Tips

  • • Possessive adjectives always come before a noun.
  • • They are different from possessive pronouns (my vs. mine).
  • • "Its" is NOT the same as "It's" (It's = it is).
