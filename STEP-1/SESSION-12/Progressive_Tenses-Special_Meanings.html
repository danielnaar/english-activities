Progressive Tenses:
Special Meanings

Some stative verbs don't usually appear in continuous tenses. But when they do, their meaning changes completely! Let's explore how their meanings shift in daily conversations.

See

vs. Seeing
Sense of sight
I see a man at the door.
Having an appointment
The doctor is seeing a patient.

Think

vs. Thinking
Opinion / Belief
What do you think he'll do?
Mental process
What is he thinking about?

Have

vs. Having
Possession
They have a car.
Eating / Experiencing
We are having lunch.

Look

vs. Looking
Appearance / Seem
You look tired.
Action of directing eyes
Why are you looking at me?

Smell

vs. Smelling
State / Characteristic
The rose smells good.
Action (sniffing)
She is smelling the flowers.

Taste

vs. Tasting
Description of flavor
This cake tastes sweet.
Action (trying flavor)
The cook is tasting the soup.

Expect

vs. Expecting
Belief / Suppose
I expect she'll pass the test.
Anticipating / Waiting for
We are expecting guests tonight.
