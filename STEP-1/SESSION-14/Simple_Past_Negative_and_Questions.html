Simple Past
Negative & Interrogative Forms

Master how to ask questions and make negative statements in the past. Remember: The auxiliary verb did does all the work!

Negative

Subject + did not (didn't) + base verb

"We didn't book the tickets."

Interrogative

Did + Subject + base verb + ?

"Did we book the tickets?"
Affirmative (Verb in Past) Negative (didn't + base) Interrogative (Did + base)
I walked in Calgary yesterday. I didn't walk in Calgary yesterday. Did I walk in Calgary yesterday?
You visited Toronto last month. You didn't visit Toronto last month. Did you visit Toronto last month?
They arrived in Quebec on Monday. They didn't arrive in Quebec on Monday. Did they arrive in Quebec on Monday?
! Move "did" to the front to make a question; add "not" after "did" for the negative.

Short Answers

  • Did she like walking around Hamilton?

    ✓ Yes, she did. | ✕ No, she didn't.

  • Did you claim the suits in Toronto?

    ✓ Yes, I did. | ✕ No, I didn't.

Exception: Verb To Be

The verb "To Be" (was/were) is strong enough to ask questions and negate by itself. Do NOT use "did".

✕ Did she be in Calgary?

✓ Was she in Calgary?

✓ I wasn't in Vancouver.

Time words that fit

yesterday last night last week in 2019 two days ago on Monday

Common Errors & Fixes

✕ They didn't arrived in Quebec.
✓ They didn't arrive in Quebec.
Fix: Always use the base verb (no -ed) after "didn't".
✕ Did you practiced winter sports?
✓ Did you practice winter sports?
Fix: Always use the base verb (no -ed) after "Did".
✕ I booked yesterday the tickets.
✓ I booked the tickets yesterday.
Fix: Time words usually go at the very end of the sentence.
