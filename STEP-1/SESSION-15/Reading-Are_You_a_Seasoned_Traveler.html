Eight signs you've graduated from "tourist" to "traveler"

60s Reading
1)

Your packing starts with the question "What can I leave behind?" and ends with a backpack that still has space. If you forget something, you buy it there, or realize you never needed it.

2)

Jet lag doesn't scare you. You've learned to walk in daylight, hydrate, and nap smart so your first day isn't wasted.

3)

You've been overcharged at least once, and you survived. Now you check fares in the app, confirm prices before the ride, and smile when you say "No, thanks."

4)

You speak "international body language": pointing, drawing a map on a napkin, counting on your fingers, and using three local words, "hello," "please," "thanks", like a pro.

5)

Plans change and you don't panic. A closed museum becomes a street-food tour; a delayed train becomes a conversation with the person next to you. Flexibility is part of the ticket.

6)

You treat reviews as hints, not commandments. You try the famous place, but you also follow the line of locals to the tiny spot around the corner.

7)

Insurance isn't a debate; it's a checklist item. You compare coverage once, buy the right plan, and stop worrying about "what ifs".

8)

You accept a beautiful truth: you'll never "see it all". That's why you keep a short, living list, three places you'd return to, and three you'll discover next.
