Future with 'going to'

A complete guide on how to express planned actions and predictions based on present evidence.

Uses

USE 1

Planned actions

We use going to when we talk about things we have already decided or prepared.

  • • She is going to visit her grandma tomorrow.
  • • They are going to play soccer after school.
USE 2

Predictions (evidence now)

We use going to when we predict something because we see it is sure or very clear.

  • • Look at those clouds! It is going to rain.
  • • She is going to be late. The bus is very slow.

Structure

✓

Affirmative

Formula

Subject + be + going to + base verb

Example

She is going to eat pizza.

✕

Negative

Formula

Subject + be + not + going to + base verb

Example

We are not going to watch TV.

?

Interrogative

Formula

Be + subject + going to + base verb?

Example

Are you going to study tonight?

Time Expressions

We often use:

tomorrow next week soon in 5 minutes tonight

"I'm going to call you tonight."
