To Have Got

"Have got" is mainly used to express possession or personal attributes.

Affirmative

+
  • • I have got a brother.
  • • I've got a cat.

Negative

−
  • • She hasn't got any pets.
  • • They have not got a car.

Questions

?
  • • Have you got a pen?
  • • Has he got any children?

Rules

■

've or 's are only used with have got, never with just have.

I've got a new phone.

■

Do not use do/does with "have got". Use have if you need auxiliary verbs.

✕ Do you have got a dog?
✓ Have you got a dog?
■

"Have got" is usually used in present simple only.

Common Mistakes

✕ Do you have got a car?
→ ✓ Have you got a car?
✕ They don't have got friends.
→ ✓ They haven't got friends.

When NOT to use

Use "have" instead of "have got" when referring to past actions or activities:

✕ I had got an accident.
✓ I had an accident.
✕ We had got lunch.
✓ We had lunch.
