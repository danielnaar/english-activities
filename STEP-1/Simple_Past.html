Simple Past

Finished actions at a specific past time. A complete guide to form, spelling, pronunciation, and common errors.

1

Form

  • Affirmative: Subject + V2 (irregular) / V + -ed (regular).
  • Negative: Subject + did not (didn't) + base verb.
  • Questions: Did + subject + base verb...?

Teaching Tip

Clap with the pattern did + base so students avoid "didn't arrived".

did + book  |  didn't + arrive

2

Time Words

yesterday last week last month last year in 2019 an hour ago on Monday

Position

At the end of the sentence or at the beginning with a comma.

"Yesterday, we arrived in Quebec."

3

Spelling Rules (regular -ed)

Rule Example
Add -ed visit → visited
Final e + d arrive → arrived, live → lived
Consonant + y → ied study → studied
Short vowel + consonant → double consonant + ed plan → planned; stop → stopped
4

Pronunciation of -ed

Ending Sound Pronunciation Examples
voiceless (p, k, f, s, sh, ch) /t/ walked /t/, asked /t/
voiced (b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r, vowels) /d/ arrived /d/, called /d/
verbs ending in t or d /ɪd/ visited /ɪd/, needed /ɪd/
5

Model Sentences (Cycle Context)

•
Affirmative We claimed the suits in Toronto yesterday.
•
Calgary offered more opportunities last year.
•
Negative They didn't arrive in Quebec yesterday.
•
He didn't visit many parks in Winnipeg.
•
Question Did she like to walk around Hamilton?
•
Did you practice winter sports in Vancouver?
6

Irregulars to Pre-Teach

Base Past Example
go went We went to Calgary last week.
see saw They saw the stadium in Hamilton.
have had She had dinner in Toronto.
make made Vancouver made a new plan.
buy bought We bought tickets online.
7

Common Errors & Fixes

✕ They didn't arrived to Quebec.
✓ They didn't arrive in Quebec.
did + base in (cities)
✕ Did you practiced winter sports?
✓ Did you practice winter sports?
✕ I booked yesterday the tickets.
✓ I booked the tickets yesterday.
time at end
8

Quick Drills (Teacher-led)

  • •
    Substitution: I booked the tickets in Calgary. → change city / time word.
  • •
    Negatives: Say it again in the negative: "She liked to walk around Hamilton."
  • •
    Questions: Turn into a question: "They arrived in Quebec yesterday."
  • •
    Pronunciation: Class chorus for -ed: /t/ /d/ /ɪd/ with examples.
