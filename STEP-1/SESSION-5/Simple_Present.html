Simple Present

We use the Simple Present to talk about habits, facts, routines, or scheduled events.

Grammar Tip

Structure: [Verb] (base form) + s / es in third person singular.

When to use it?

USE 1

Habits & Actions

Things we do regularly or things that usually happen.

  • I play tennis.
  • She does not play tennis.
  • Does he play tennis?
  • She always forgets her purse.
USE 2

Facts & Truths

To express what we think is true now, in the past, and future.

  • Cats like milk.
  • California is in America.
  • Windows are made of glass.
USE 3

Scheduled Events

Use it for public schedules like trains, classes or events.

  • The train leaves at 8 AM.
  • The party starts at 8 o'clock.
  • When does class begin tomorrow?

Forms of the Simple Present

✓

Affirmative

  • • I work in the factory.
  • • She lives in Brazil.
  • • They play soccer.
✕

Negative

  • • I don't dance well.
  • • She doesn't want to go back.
  • • We don't eat meat.
?

Interrogative

  • • Do you play tennis?
  • • Does he play the piano?
  • • Do they study every day?

Key Reminders

•

He / She / It adds -s or -es to the verb.

•

Use do / does for questions.

•

Use don't / doesn't for negatives.
