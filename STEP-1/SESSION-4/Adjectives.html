Vocabulary Building:
Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns. They tell us what something looks like, feels like, sounds like, tastes like, and much more.

They help make our language richer and more specific.

Remember: Adjectives usually go before the noun.

For example: a red apple, a tall building.

Categories of Adjectives

Appearance

adorable beautiful clean elegant fancy handsome

Color

red orange yellow green blue purple

Condition

better careful clever dead easy famous

Feelings (Bad)

angry embarrassed grumpy helpless

Feelings (Good)

agreeable brave calm gentle happy

Shape

curved deep flat high

Size

big fat gigantic great huge

Sound

loud melodic noisy purring quiet

Time

brief early fast late long

Taste

bitter delicious fresh greasy juicy

Quantity

abundant empty full many numerous

Examples in Context

The tall man is wearing a blue shirt.

I had a delicious meal last night.

These noisy kids are running everywhere!

She found a clean and elegant dress.
