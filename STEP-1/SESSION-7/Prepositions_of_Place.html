Prepositions of Place

Use at for a point, in for an enclosed space, and on for a surface.

AT

(a point)

•at the door
•at the bus stop
•at the corner
•at the top of the page
•at the end of the road
•at the entrance
•at the crossroads
•at the front desk

IN

(an enclosed space)

•in the garden
•in a building
•in my pocket
•in a box
•in my wallet
•in London
•in France
•in a car

ON

(a surface)

•on the wall
•on the floor
•on the ceiling
•on the carpet
•on the door
•on the cover
•on the menu
•on a page
