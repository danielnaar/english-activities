A Night in the Ice Hotel

Read the following text carefully. Pay attention to the details and new vocabulary.

Every winter in Jukkasjärvi, Sweden, something magical happens. A massive hotel is built entirely out of snow and ice from the nearby Torne River. It is known as the Ice Hotel, and it melts away every spring, only to be rebuilt the following winter with a completely new and spectacular design.

Inside the hotel, the temperature is kept at a constant -5°C (23°F) to prevent the ice from melting. Guests sleep on beds made of solid ice, but they don't freeze! The beds are covered with thick reindeer skins, and visitors sleep inside extreme thermal sleeping bags to stay warm. The hotel features beautiful ice sculptures, an ice bar, and even an ice chapel for weddings.

Staying at the Ice Hotel is a unique experience. There is no heating, no television, and no en-suite bathrooms inside the ice rooms. Despite the freezing temperatures, visitors describe the profound silence and the crisp air as incredibly peaceful. It is a true adventure for those looking to escape the ordinary.
