Much, Many,
A lot of, Lots of

Learn how to express large quantities correctly based on whether a noun is countable or uncountable.

1. Use "Much"

with uncountable nouns

  • ✓
    Interrogative How much petrol is in the car?
  • ✓
    Negative We don't have much time left.
  • ✕
    Wrong I have much friends.

2. Use "Many"

with countable nouns

  • ✓
    Interrogative How many cookies did you eat?
  • ✓
    Negative Not many students passed the test.
  • ✕
    Wrong There is many sugar in the coffee.

3. A lot of / Lots of

Use with both!

They both mean "a large quantity". Use them freely with countable and uncountable nouns!

  • ✓
    Countable A lot of cars are stuck in traffic.
  • ✓
    Uncountable She drank lots of water.

Pro Tip

A lot of is more common in writing. Lots of is more casual or spoken.

"We had lots of fun!"

vs.

"There was a lot of noise at the party."
